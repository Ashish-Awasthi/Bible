The wilderness quest includes the laws and instructions
given in Leviticus and Numbers. After convincing the
stubborn people to agree to further conditions, Moses
dies at the end of Deuteronomy. The Jordan dries up and
the Israelites enter the promised land in Joshua 3.

Exodus 26-28 lays out the plans for the tabernacle
and is the first time Aaron and his sons wear their holy
garments. Their role as custodians of the law becomes
official in Leviticus 8. Jericho falls in Joshua 6.

The Book of Judges describes the conflicts Israel faced
with the people already living in the land. Deborah
directs the defeat of the Canaanite army in chapter 4.

Gideon meets the angel in Judges 6 and subdues the
Midianites in chapter 8. Samson is born at the end of
Judges 13 and loses his hair three chapters later.

The Book of Ruth is just four chapters and has a happy
ending. God answers Hannah’s prayer in 1 Samuel 1 and
calls the boy to be His prophet in chapter 3.

By asking for a human king, the people fail to see that
God Himself is king. Samuel warns that kings will only
tax them and turn them into slaves in 1 Samuel 8.

The most handsome man in all Israel (1 Samuel 9), Saul
is anointed prince by Samuel and hailed king by the
people in chapter 10. The chapters that follow show how
he quickly loses their favour as well as his own sanity.

In 1 Samuel 16, God sends Samuel south to Bethlehem in
Judah, where he anoints Jesse’s youngest son with oil.
The showdown with Goliath quickly follows in chapter 17.

After a tense twenty chapters, Saul falls on his own
sword and David becomes king over Israel and Judah
(2 Samuel 5). Solomon, son of David and Bathsheba,
begins his reign in 1 Kings 1. Chapters 6-7 recount the
seven years it took to build the temple, compared to the
thirteen years Solomon devoted to the royal palace.
