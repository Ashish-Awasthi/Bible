About translations

The Bible was first written in Hebrew and Aramaic and then translated into
Greek and Latin. Many of the original words, phrases and stories have confused
scholars for centuries. Because modern English is so different from ancient
Hebrew, there are a variety of ways to express what the Bible says.

English Bibles can be found on the internet by including their titles and “online
Bible” in your search terms. Biblos.com offers a comprehensive selection that
includes an interlinear tool that allows you to see the translation word by word.

Here are some examples of the best-known publications.

Jewish Publication Society

The JPS 1917 version can be found at breslov.com and is
sensitive to the orthodox respect for the divine name.
The more recent edition is called the Tanakh, a short way
of stating its contents: Torah, Prophets and Writings.

King James Version

This was the third official English translation, authorized by James II for the
Church of England in 1611. As a Christian Bible, it includes the New Testament.
Although people from many denominations admire its elegant phrasings, the
old-fashioned style can prove a challenge to understand.

Douay-Rheims

Catholics also produced an English Bible, which they translated from Saint
Jerome’s Latin version of the Bible called the Vulgate. This means the English is
two steps away from the original Hebrew. It is less commonly read today but still
the preferred version of Scripture by traditional Catholics.

Revised Standard Version

First published in the decade after World War II, the RSV aimed to provide more
accurate ways of expressing Old Testament Hebrew. It is similar to the
Jerusalem Bible and other modern editions for sounding more like the language
we speak today. A “New” RSV adapts phrases like “sons of Israel” to “children of
Israel” so that all readers feel included.

Good News Bible

Also known as the Good News Translation,
this conveys the Hebrew “thought for
thought” rather than “word for word”. Some
find that original meanings can get lost in this
process, but its simple style is easy to read.
